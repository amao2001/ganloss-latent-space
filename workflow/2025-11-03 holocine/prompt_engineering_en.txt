### Part 3: Shot Sequence Description (The Narrative "Storyboard")

Each shot description, starting from `[per shot caption]`, must be an **independent, specific cinematic instruction** that collectively drives the narrative or emotional development.

1.  **Shot Type and Angle:**
    *   Each description must include a clear shot size (e.g., **Close-up**, **Medium Shot**, **Wide Shot**, **Extreme Close-up**, **Medium Close-up**).
    *   It should include a clear camera angle (e.g., **Low-angle shot**, **High-angle shot**, **Over-the-shoulder shot**).
    *   If necessary, use a **Point-of-view shot**.
2.  **Action and Emotion:**
    *   Clearly describe the **specific action** and **current emotion** of the character in the shot. The actions should be coherent.

3.  **Narrative Progression and Focus:**
    *   The shot sequence should exhibit a clear **narrative arc** (e.g., from a wide shot establishing the environment to a close-up focusing on emotion; from the beginning of a conflict to the emergence of a turning point).

4.  **Consistency and Continuity:**
